§ 02 — Voice

We sound like the operators we are.

Confident without bluster. Specific without jargon. Fast without sloppy. Our voice is a senior partner across the table from a founder, not a brand voice document trying to sound like one.

↑ We are
  • Specific
  • Direct
  • Calmly confident
  • Editorial
  • In on the joke
↓ We are not
  • Buzzy
  • Bombastic
  • Performatively edgy
  • Synergistic
  • Best-in-class
House rules
  1. 01. Lead with the news, not the framing.
  2. 02. Earn every adjective. Cut the rest.
  3. 03. Numbers over adverbs.
  4. 04. Say the obvious thing if it's true.
  5. 05. Never use italics for emphasis. Use weight or color.
Tagline lockup

Clarity from the trenches.
Powered by NorthTK.

The two-line lockup is our most-used external tagline. It can stand alone or appear above a product shot, a stat, or a hero image. Pair line one with North Blue; reserve orange and yellow for editorial punctuation elsewhere on the page.

01 Primary palette · Coral P. 06 02 Secondary palette P. 07 03 Per-color systems P. 08 04 Usage rules P. 09
A four-color palette on warm parchment

Parchment is the page. Ink is the body. Coral is the primary accent — it carries the website, the principal lockup, and the hero surfaces. Orange and yellow are editorial punctuation: pulled in for emphasis, never combined.

The palette is small on purpose. A consultancy that promises clarity should look like one. Every additional color is a tax on attention.

§ 04.04 — Usage rules

Use the palette like punctuation.

A small set of bright accents, applied with restraint. The palette is doing its job when most of the page is parchment, ink and white — and the color lands where attention should.

↗ Do
✕ Don't
→ One accent per surface

Coral, orange, or yellow — pick one. They never appear together in the same composition.

→ Parchment leads, paper supports

Parchment is the default page tone. Reserve paper white for product surfaces, dashboards, and forms.

→ Lapis belongs to NorthTK

Never use the product blue on Title TK marketing — it signals software, not consultancy.

§ 05.03 — Type in use

Three notes the system can hit.

Reckless and Plex don't compete for the spotlight — they trade it. Display sets the position; mono adds the rigor; Plex Sans does the work in between.

→ No italics

Use weight or color for emphasis. Italics belong to other systems.

→ No drop shadows on type

If contrast is failing, fix the surface — not the letterforms.

→ Mono for metadata only

Plex Mono is structural — eyebrows, captions, codes. Never body copy.

→ Display = roman, single weight

One weight per headline. No mixing 700 and 800 in the same line.
