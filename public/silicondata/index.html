OVERVIEW
THE SITUATION

Overview.

SiliconData has established product-market fit in a sector experiencing sustained demand growth. Carmen Li, formerly a data executive at Bloomberg and trader at DRW, brings domain expertise in financial infrastructure and market transparency. Her Ph.D. in Mathematical Physics from the University of Edinburgh positions her to translate technical performance metrics into actionable market intelligence.

The company's mission centers on creating the pricing and performance standard for AI compute markets. By tracking GPU rental costs (Nvidia H100, B200) and performance variations, SiliconData provides transparency in a market where pricing remains opaque and fragmented. This model mirrors Bloomberg's role in financial markets: converting proprietary data collection into subscription intelligence products.

Li's approach to company building reflects operational discipline. Silicon Data scaled revenue 5x between January and April 2026 without formal sales infrastructure or dedicated product management. The company secured backing from DRW and Jump Trading, firms that understand data-as-infrastructure businesses. This investor profile suggests confidence in the underlying data asset rather than speculative technology hype.

The broader market context supports SiliconData's timing. As AI compute becomes a traded commodity, buyers require benchmarking tools to evaluate vendor claims and arbitrage pricing inefficiencies. Companies making multi-million-dollar GPU commitments need independent verification of performance and cost structures. The absence of an established category leader creates an opening for a player who can build credibility through rigorous methodology and consistent reporting.

NARRATIVE ARCHITECTURE

Themes and
storylines.

Four narrative pillars that position SiliconData as the independent measurement standard for GPU compute pricing.

01
Trust: Measurement credibility requires independence. SiliconData operates as a neutral third party with no stake in GPU sales or cloud platform revenue. Funded by compute buyers who need transparency, the company's incentive structure aligns with accurate reporting rather than vendor promotion. This positioning mirrors Bloomberg's independence in financial markets — data integrity over distribution relationships.
02
Comparability: Enterprise GPU buyers allocate budgets across hyperscalers, neoclouds, and spot markets without standardized benchmarks. SiliconData's normalized pricing methodology enables apples-to-apples comparison across providers, geographies, and lease structures. When a CFO evaluates a $10M compute commitment, comparable data transforms guesswork into informed capital allocation.
03
Adaptability: As compute liquidity grows, markets evolve — new rental structures emerge, pricing mechanisms shift, regional supply dynamics change. SiliconData's infrastructure is built to scale with market complexity. The platform tracks 50+ providers across 50 countries, capturing pricing variations as they happen. When the market invents new trading instruments or pricing models, the measurement layer adapts in real time.
04
Category Leadership: Every mature market crowns a measurement standard. GPU compute is reaching that inflection point. The company that establishes methodological rigor and reporter trust first becomes the default citation — the S&P of AI infrastructure. Speed determines who owns the category.
THE PROGRAM

Program.

SiliconData has a credible point of view, proprietary data and insights, and a moment in the market that makes it timely and highly relevant. The program operates on the Nielsen model for data storytelling: quick-turn pitching based on market signals captured in real time. When B200 prices spike, when H100 spot rates soften, when hyperscaler CapEx guidance shifts — we move from data to story in hours, not days.

PHASE 1

Foundation

Build the narrative infrastructure and trust positioning.

  • Data audit: Map SiliconData's existing content corpus (weekly blogs, market reports, pricing indices)
  • Methodology storytelling: Build trust narrative around normalization framework, breadth of coverage, independence positioning
  • Category narrative architecture: Establish "GPU compute's S&P" positioning across founder, company, and category storylines
  • Spokesperson preparation: CEO media training for tier-one financial interviews
  • Proof library assembly: Verified pricing records, coverage breadth stats, methodology transparency materials
PHASE 2

Infrastructure

Build the signal-to-story machine.

  • Signal-to-story system: Build the machine that converts dashboard data into pitch-ready narratives
  • Media target map: Tier-one financial reporters, semiconductor analysts, cloud infrastructure beat
  • Press materials foundation: Boilerplate language, exec bios, data visualization standards
  • Reporter relationship mapping: Identify who covers hyperscaler CapEx, GPU allocation, data center economics
  • Embargo protocol: Structure for exclusive data releases to tier-one outlets
PHASE 3

Velocity

Activate weekly data storytelling cadence.

  • Weekly data storytelling cadence: Active pitching tied to market movements
  • Tier-one financial media activation: WSJ, Bloomberg, FT, Reuters, CNBC placements
  • Analyst briefing program: Gartner, IDC, Forrester introductions
  • Thought leadership pipeline: CEO bylines, podcast interviews, conference panel submissions
  • Momentum measurement: Track coverage, analyst citations, client inbound attribution
