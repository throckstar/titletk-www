How We Work 05

A process built
for speed.

Most agencies have a process designed to protect their timeline. Ours is designed to compress it. Four phases. Clear deliverables at each step. Results before the engagement is finished — not after a six-month ramp.

Phase 01 · Weeks 1–3
Get Smart.
Phase 02 · Weeks 4–6
Shape.
Phase 03 · Weeks 7–10
Roll Out.
Phase 04 · Weeks 11–13
Engage.
01

Get Smart.

Founder and team interviews. Audit of existing materials, messaging, and media coverage. Competitive landscape mapping — what Orgill, True Value, and the home improvement trade press are saying about the category, and where the gaps are. Market signal analysis through North TK. The deliverable at the end of this phase is a Story Diagnostic: an honest read of where Do It Best's narrative is working, where it isn't, and what the highest-leverage opportunity is heading into the build phase.

Stakeholder Interviews Materials Audit Competitive Landscape North TK Signal Capture Deliverable: Story Diagnostic
02

Shape.

Narrative intelligence — the core messaging architecture built from what we learned in Phase 01. This is where we develop the master narrative, the messaging hierarchy, and the voice guide that everything else runs through. Proof points. Elevator copy. The language that makes the Do It Best technology story land differently in a trade press interview than in a member communication. We test it against reporter reaction before we lock it.

Narrative Intelligence Messaging Hierarchy Voice Guide Proof Points Deliverable: Narrative Brief
03

Roll Out.

Execution starts. Pitches, talk tracks, and sales enablement assets built on the narrative we just locked. Executive prep for live moments — whether that's trade press interviews, industry panels, or member communications. Stress-tests in real conditions: early media conversations, byline drafts, or a keynote session at Do It Best's own dealer market. The story goes live under controlled conditions so we can see what's working before we push the volume up.

Media Pitches Talk Tracks Sales Enablement Executive Prep Deliverable: Story Kit
04

Engage.

Earned media, owned content, and executive visibility running at full strength. Trade press relationships activated. Bylined articles, leadership content, and social amplification working together. Coverage tracked against the storylines we built, not just volume. Distribution measured and refined. The goal at this phase isn't a launch moment — it's a media presence that builds on itself, where every piece of coverage creates the conditions for the next one.

Earned Media Owned Content Executive Visibility Distribution & Measurement Deliverable: Coverage Report

About Title TK

Title TK is a strategic communications firm for growth-stage technology companies. Small, senior, and operator-led, we embed with clients rather than service them from a distance. Our work drives three outcomes: Recognition (building awareness in your market), Relevance (owning your category), and Revenue (growing the business through narrative that performs). We help clients succeed in any climate by putting the entrepreneur first and grounding solutions in results.

Title TK was founded by Cristina Pena (co-led the emerging tech practice and launched and led the B2B2C practice at Spark), Jason Throckmorton (co-founded the award-winning marketing and communications agency LaunchSquad), and Mike Barash (founded and led Codeword through its acquisition by Waggener Edstrom).
